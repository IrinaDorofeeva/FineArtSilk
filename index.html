What Inspires Me? How Do I Make It?

Usually I find inspiration in nature around me. I observe, I listen, I explore and paint. I love to learn new painting skills, but Serti silk painting technique will be always my favorite one.

Irina Dorofeeva

Artist

I always liked to paint and work, as well, in other forms of creative art.
I took different art classes and workshops in different media during my life, but when I discovered the world of silk painting (2002) it quickly became my passion. Since then I spent all my free time painting on silk. Some of my work is an abstraction comming from reality around me, from my experience; other work is imaginary and improvisational. My studies and experiences have given me a broad range of skills, allowing me to express my artistic vision.

Statement

Silk painting has been a primary fascination for me over the most part of my life.
I enjoy exploring the diverse effects that come when I apply paint to silk. This, further, sparks my creativity. Some of my works focus on nature and are inspired by landscapes, other works are more imaginary. I am interested in composition, in arranging shapes and colors, in exploring the diversity of forms. I find that silk painting is the best medium to do that. In this manner, I try to connect with the viewer through my work. I try to share the pleasure of discovering beauty in the world around me.

Serti Technique

The particular technique that I use is called “the Serti technique”.
Serti ( is a French word for "fence" or "enclosing") is the silk painting technique where designs are formed with gutta or resist liner. I studied this process in Russia, the country which is known for cultivating the Serti technique and introducing it to Western Europe. Indeed this technique, so popular nowadays in France, came to Paris through members of the Russian Czar's family. During the Bolshevik Revolution, members of the Czar's family dispersed throughout Europe. Many fled to Paris, bringing with them the secrets of the Serti silk painting.
